Bonus Answer — B2B Editing
What do I like — and what would I change?

What I like: B2B video that respects the viewer's intelligence. The best SaaS ads don't over-explain — they show the pain, sit in it for a second, then let the product land as relief. That restraint is what makes them memorable.

What I'd change: Most B2B content still leads with features, not feelings. "Here's what our platform does" instead of "here's the problem you've been silently dealing with." I'd push Coursebox's content to lead with the human cost of bad onboarding before mentioning AI or courses — make the viewer nod before you sell anything.

On pacing: short form B2B moves too slow. The first 2 seconds need to earn the next 8. I'd tighten cuts, use silence deliberately, and trust the viewer to keep up.
